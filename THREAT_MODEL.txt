# Threat Model (DataOfInsta)

## Assets
- Your Instagram account integrity (credentials, sessions)
- Personal data you expose via profile/content
- Recovery mechanisms (email/phone)

## Threat actors
- Impersonators (catfishing)
- Scammers/phishers
- Credential-stuffing attackers
- Session hijackers (malware or stolen cookies)
- Malicious “analyzer” tools attempting data harvesting

## Attack surfaces
- Public profile fields (bio, links, highlights)
- Visible content patterns (posting times/locations)
- Connected apps / weak email security
- Social engineering via DMs and login prompts
- Any automated tool you run that could be harvesting secrets

## Assumptions
- Users are reviewing their own account or have explicit permission.
- Analysis is consent-based and avoids unauthorized access.

## Security goals
- Prevent the repository/tool from collecting credentials or secrets
- Provide guidance to reduce exposure and improve account hardening
- Encourage responsible disclosure

## Non-goals
- Hacking Instagram
- Unauthorized scraping
- Bypassing access controls

## Mitigations (design requirements)
- Documentation-first (no credential access)
- Privacy-preserving workflows
- Redaction-by-default in any reporting templates
- Clear ethics and scope statements
